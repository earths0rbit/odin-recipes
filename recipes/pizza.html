Traditional Napoletana Pizza Recipe Using Biga

The traditional Napoletana pizza made with biga features a light, airy crust with a slightly chewy texture and a crispy, golden-brown exterior. The biga, a pre-fermented dough, imparts a subtle depth of flavor and enhances the overall dough structure. The pizza is topped with a simple yet flavorful combination of San Marzano tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil leaves, and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. The result is a beautifully balanced pizza with a harmonious blend of fresh, high-quality ingredients and a deliciously authentic taste.

Ingredients

For the biga

  • 500g Italian "00" flour
  • 220g water (44% hydration)
  • 2g fresh yeast

For the dough

  • 500g Italian "00" flour
  • 300g water (60% hydration)
  • 15g salt
  • 3g fresh yeast

For the toppings

  • 400g canned San Marzano tomatoes
  • 250g fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt to taste

Instructions

Preparing the Biga

  1. Dissolve the fresh yeast in water.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flour with the yeast-water mixture until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it ferment at room temperature for 16-24 hours.

Preparing the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, dissolve 3g of fresh yeast in 300g of water.
  2. Add the prepared biga to the water and mix until it breaks up and disperses in the water.
  3. Gradually add the 500g of "00" flour to the mixture, stirring until a dough begins to form.
  4. Add the salt and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic, about 10-15 minutes by hand or 8-10 minutes with a stand mixer.
  5. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough into 4 equal portions (about 250g each) and shape them into tight balls.
  7. Place the dough balls on a floured surface, cover with a damp cloth, and let them rise for 4-6 hours at room temperature.

Preparing the Sauce

  1. Crush the canned San Marzano tomatoes by hand or with a blender until you get a smooth consistency.
  2. Add a pinch of sea salt to taste. No need to cook the sauce.

Assembling and Baking the Pizza

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible (250°C/482°F or higher if possible) and place a pizza stone or steel in the oven to heat up for at least 45 minutes.
  2. On a floured surface, take one dough ball and press it down with your fingers, gently stretching it into a 10-12 inch circle. Leave the edges slightly thicker for the crust.
  3. Transfer the stretched dough onto a floured pizza peel or parchment paper.
  4. Spread a thin layer of the tomato sauce over the dough, leaving about an inch around the edges.
  5. Distribute the sliced mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.
  6. Add a few fresh basil leaves on top.
  7. Drizzle a small amount of extra virgin olive oil over the pizza.

Baking

  1. Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or steel in the oven.
  2. Bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly charred.
  3. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing.

Serving

  1. Drizzle a little more extra virgin olive oil on top of the pizza.
  2. Garnish with additional fresh basil leaves if desired.
  3. Serve hot and enjoy your authentic Napoletana pizza!
